What is Contrast?

White Space is Not Your Enemy definition

"Contrast is an important principle for designing interesting (as opposed to boring) layouts. Contrast, as a principle, offers a great deal of flexibility. There are limitless ways to achieve it."(pg 56)

"Start with the elements of design. You can employ contrast between filled and empty space. You can employ contrasting sorts of lines or shapes. You may juxtapose contrasting sizes of objects. Introducing a pattern in proximity to no pattern results in contrast. Ditto for texture. Or you may contrast two different kinds of patterns or two different kinds of textures. Color and value also offer powerful contrast tools. Using both dark and light values or colors result in contrast."(pg 56)

White Space is Not Your Enemy by Rebecca Hagen and Kim Golombisky. Published in 2017 by Taylor and Francis Group, LLC.

Personal Definition

It is important to add contrast to your work to avoid a boring design. Adding contrast makes a design more appealing. A viewer is more likely to be intrigued by an exciting piece of work rather than a boring one. If you work with your colors, shapes and sizes you are able to contrast within your design.

Light elements on dark backgrounds create a sense of contrast. You can also use different sizes of elements to make the image have more contrast.
